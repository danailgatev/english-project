Research on School Successful Celebrities

10 remarkable personalities who started their journey at SMG

Sofia Mathematical High School "Paisius of Hilendar" (SMG) is widely recognized as one of the most prestigious and successful secondary schools in Bulgaria. For decades, it has been educating some of the brightest young minds in the country and has produced a remarkable number of outstanding professionals in the fields of mathematics, computer science, physics, medicine, business, and culture. Many of its graduates have gone on to achieve significant success both in Bulgaria and on the international stage.

This article is presenting the stories behind some of the most well-known personalities who graduated from SMG. There are many celebrities who have made important contributions in their respective fields, but the research follows only 10 different lives of theirs. Through their examples, we can see how the strong academic foundation, rigorous education, and unique environment of Sofia Mathematical High School They have played a significant role in shaping their future achievements and careers.

Maria Ilieva – Singer, Songwriter, and Producer

Maria Ilieva, a proud graduate of Sofia Mathematical High School, stands as one of the most successful and influential female pop artists in contemporary Bulgarian music. Born into a family of classical musicians in Veliko Tarnovo, she began her musical journey early, taking piano and solfeggio lessons from the age of five and developing her voice in prestigious choirs. After moving to Sofia, she completed her secondary education at SMG, where the rigorous analytical training and discipline she acquired laid a strong foundation for her future success in both creative and professional endeavors. She later earned a degree in insurance business from the University of National and World Economy and a master’s degree in international relations, showcasing her intellectual versatility.

Maria started her career as a vocalist with the band “Visoko” and gained early television experience hosting music shows on CG TV and BNT. In 2000, she joined the group Kaffe before launching a highly successful solo career under a contract with Bulgarian Music Company. Over more than two decades as a solo artist, she has released over 30 hit singles that consistently topped national charts, along with two award-winning studio albums, a maxi single, and a greatest hits compilation. She has received more than 40 prestigious awards for her music and personal style, cementing her status as a leading figure in Bulgarian pop. GRAZIA magazine named her Woman of the Year five times (in 2006, 2009, 2011, 2013, and 2018), while Forbes Bulgaria recognized her as the most influential female celebrity for three consecutive years. Maria has also made a mark as a television personality, serving as a judge on the first three seasons of X Factor Bulgaria, the first season of The Masked Singer Bulgaria, and seasons 10 and 11 of The Voice of Bulgaria. The logical precision and systematic approach she developed at SMG have clearly supported her success as a songwriter, producer, and business-minded artist who manages a long and sustainable career.

Nikol Stankulova – Weather Presenter

Nikol Stankulova, an accomplished graduate of Sofia Mathematical High School, has become one of the most recognizable and beloved television personalities in Bulgaria. From a remarkably young age she demonstrated natural charisma and a passion for public communication. She won her first beauty title at the age of six and began appearing on Bulgarian National Television (BNT) when she was only seven, marking the start of a multifaceted media career. After completing her secondary education at SMG, where she honed her analytical thinking, precision, and ability to process information quickly, Nikol pursued higher education in finance at the University of National and World Economy. This combination of mathematical discipline and economic knowledge provided her with a solid foundation for clear and confident on-screen performance. Nikol gained nationwide popularity as the weather presenter in the popular morning block “Hello, Bulgaria” on bTV. Her warm, engaging style and clear delivery transformed routine weather forecasts into an enjoyable and informative part of viewers’ daily routines. She has also hosted other programs, including “The Big Question” on Nova TV, and worked as a news anchor for digital platforms such as Sportal.bg and Profit.bg. In addition to her television work, Nikol built a successful modeling career and demonstrated her adaptability by reaching the final in the reality show VIP Brother Bulgaria. Throughout her career she has balanced multiple roles — television host, model, and public figure — while maintaining a professional yet approachable image that resonates with audiences of all ages. The logical mindset and attention to detail she developed at SMG have been particularly valuable in live broadcasting, where accuracy and composure under pressure are essential. Nikol’s long-term presence in the national media has made her a trusted face in Bulgarian households. She continues to inspire young women to pursue diverse careers that combine intellect, communication skills, and personal presence.

Ivo Andreev – Weather Presenter

Ivo Andreev, a distinguished graduate of Sofia Mathematical High School, has built a long and respected career that successfully bridges scientific expertise in meteorology with effective television communication. After finishing SMG, where the demanding curriculum strengthened his logical reasoning and analytical skills, he studied physics and meteorology at Sofia University “St. Kliment Ohridski”. He gained valuable professional experience working as a synoptic for the Bulgarian Air Force and at the National Institute of Meteorology and Hydrology, developing deep knowledge of atmospheric processes and forecasting techniques. Ivo began his television career at a young age and has presented weather forecasts on both bTV and Nova TV, becoming a familiar and trusted voice for Bulgarian viewers. His calm demeanor, clear explanations, and accurate information help audiences plan their daily activities with greater confidence.

In addition to regular weather segments, he has hosted other television formats, showcasing his versatility as a presenter. Over several decades in broadcasting, Ivo has maintained high professional standards and adapted successfully to changes in the media landscape while keeping scientific accuracy at the core of his work. The systematic thinking and problem-solving abilities he cultivated at SMG have been instrumental in both his scientific work and on-screen performance. These skills allow him to translate complex meteorological data into accessible and engaging content for the general public. Ivo continues to enjoy respect from colleagues and viewers alike for his reliability and professionalism.

Angel Dzhambazki – Member of the European Parliament

Angel Dzhambazki, a graduate of Sofia Mathematical High School, has achieved notable success in Bulgarian and European politics through dedication, analytical strength, and consistent public engagement. After completing his studies at SMG, where he developed rigorous logical reasoning and structured thinking, he earned a law degree from Sofia University “St. Kliment Ohridski.” This combination of mathematical precision and legal knowledge equipped him well for navigating complex political environments.

Angel became actively involved in VMRO – Bulgarian National Movement early in his career and rose through the ranks, eventually serving as Deputy Chairman of the party. From 2007 to 2014 he worked as a Sofia Municipal Councilor, gaining practical experience in local governance and addressing issues relevant to the capital’s residents. In 2014 he was elected as a Member of the European Parliament and was successfully re-elected in 2019, serving until 2024 as part of the European Conservatives and Reformists group. During his decade in the European Parliament, Angel actively participated in debates and contributed to discussions on topics of importance to Bulgaria, including national identity, regional cooperation, migration policies, and the protection of Bulgarian communities abroad. The analytical discipline and systematic approach he acquired at SMG supported his effectiveness in parliamentary work and political advocacy. He has been a consistent voice for positions held by many Bulgarians on the European stage. Angel’s long-term commitment to public service highlights how a strong educational foundation from Sofia Mathematical High School can prepare individuals for influential roles in both national and international politics.

Ivo Tonev – Football Executive

Ivo Tonev, a graduate of Sofia Mathematical High School, has made notable contributions to Bulgarian football as a businessman, former professional player, and sports executive. He served as Executive Director and later Chairman of the Board of PFC Levski Sofia, one of Bulgaria’s most historic and successful clubs, where he worked to improve management, financial stability, and overall competitiveness during challenging periods. In his role at Levski, he addressed critical issues such as player wages, training conditions, and club operations, often making tough decisions to ensure the club’s future. Previously, he held leadership positions at Lokomotiv Sofia and was Chairman of the Bulgarian Sports Totalizator, contributing to the broader sports administration landscape in the country.

As a Sofia Municipal Councilor from 2007 to 2012, Ivo brought his experience into local governance, linking sports with public policy. His economic education from the University of National and World Economy, paired with the strong analytical discipline developed at SMG, helped him navigate complex organizational and financial challenges in professional football. Throughout his career, Ivo demonstrated strategic vision and commitment to elevating Bulgarian club football on both domestic and international levels. He was involved in key moments, including player contract negotiations, coach appointments, and efforts to combat issues like racism in stadiums. His practical background as a former footballer gave him unique insight into the needs of players and teams. Ivo Tonev’s work has left a lasting mark on the management of major Bulgarian sports institutions. The logical and systematic thinking fostered at Sofia Mathematical High School clearly supported his success in the demanding world of sports administration and business. He continues to serve as an inspiring example for young SMG graduates interested in combining analytical skills with leadership in dynamic fields like professional sports.

Ivo Indzhev – Bulgarian Journalist

Ivo Indzhev, a graduate of Sofia Mathematical High School, is a highly respected Bulgarian journalist, blogger, author, and former television presenter with a remarkable international career. He served as Director General of the Bulgarian Telegraph Agency (BTA) from 1990 to 1993, guiding the agency through a critical transition period after major political changes in Bulgaria. Earlier, he worked as a BTA correspondent in Beirut, Lebanon from 1983 to 1987, gaining valuable international experience in conflict zones and foreign reporting. Ivo was Chief Editor of influential newspapers such as “Express” and “Democracy,” shaping public discourse during important historical moments. He hosted the popular and impactful program “In the Top Ten” on bTV from 2000 to 2006, where his sharp analysis and direct style addressed key political and social issues. Known for his independence, Ivo was involved in high-profile moments, including confronting authorities on matters of corruption and media freedom. He later became a member of the European Union’s Eastern Strategic Communication Team and continued writing books and maintaining a widely read blog that analyzes politics, society, and current events. Fluent in several languages, he has built a reputation as a thoughtful and outspoken voice in Bulgarian journalism. His analytical mindset, nurtured during his years at SMG, has been a cornerstone of his ability to dissect complex topics with clarity and depth. Ivo’s long career spans traditional media, television, and digital platforms, demonstrating adaptability and commitment to independent journalism. He remains an influential figure whose work encourages critical thinking among readers and viewers. The rigorous education at Sofia Mathematical High School provided him with the logical tools essential for high-level investigative and analytical reporting. Ivo Indzhev stands as a proud example of how SMG graduates can achieve significant success in the demanding field of media and public commentary.

Several of her compositions have supported the careers of prominent Bulgarian pop and classical performers, contributing meaningfully to the national repertoire. Throughout her career, Zornitsa has participated in cultural initiatives that enrich Bulgaria’s artistic heritage. The discipline and systematic approach she acquired at Sofia Mathematical High School have enhanced her ability to develop coherent and impactful musical works. She continues to compose and inspire younger musicians with her thoughtful and professional approach. Zornitsa Popova serves as an excellent example of how graduates of SMG can successfully bridge analytical education with artistic accomplishment, bringing diversity and depth to Bulgaria’s cultural landscape through their creative endeavors.

Through humor and authenticity, Atanas Mishev continues to build a strong online community and inspire other young creators in Bulgaria.

Boris Mihov – Computer Scientist

Boris Mihov, a graduate of Sofia Mathematical High School, has excelled as a talented Bulgarian computer scientist and competitive programmer with impressive achievements in informatics. He began programming at a young age and quickly rose through national and international competitions, earning multiple medals at the International Olympiad in Informatics (IOI), including bronze medals that highlight his problem-solving excellence. Boris has represented Bulgaria successfully in several IOI editions, demonstrating outstanding skills in algorithms, data structures, and computational thinking. His strong foundation in mathematics from SMG provided the perfect base for advanced studies and competitive success in computer science. He has participated actively in programming contests on platforms like Codeforces and continues to develop his expertise in the field. Boris has been recognized as a GTF BIG Talent Scholar, acknowledging his potential and dedication to informatics. His achievements include consistent high-level performances from early school years through upper grades at SMG. The rigorous training at Sofia Mathematical High School clearly played a crucial role in sharpening his logical and analytical abilities. Boris serves as an inspiring role model for younger students interested in technology and competitive programming. He exemplifies how a mathematical high school education can lead to remarkable accomplishments in the rapidly evolving world of computer science and informatics. Through his competition results and ongoing contributions, Boris Mihov continues to bring pride to SMG and Bulgarian programming talent on the global stage.
